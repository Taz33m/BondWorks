Dealer selection is the first execution decision.

BondWorks Lite scores counterparties before an RFQ is sent, using simulated price quality, response rate, latency, sector hit rate, size capacity, and inventory fit.

The trader sends a five-million-dollar RFQ for a ten-year Treasury and watches dealer liquidity form in real time.

Quotes arrive with price, yield, spread, size, latency, and status. The best offer is highlighted, slow dealers are visible, and expired or missing responses stay traceable.

If the trader chooses a non-best quote, BondWorks requires a best-execution rationale before execution.

After the fill, the analytics page compares the execution against the best quote, cover price, mid, last print, and simulated tape VWAP.

The audit trail records every lifecycle event, from RFQ creation through rationale, trade creation, and analytics generation.

Underneath the workstation is a serious backend: Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Redis, Redpanda, WebSockets, and a Python strategy API.
